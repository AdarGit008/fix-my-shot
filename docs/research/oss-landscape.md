# OSS Landscape — form grading, shot simulation, and reusable pose/physics code

**Status:** research record · **Date:** 2026-07-21 · **Method:** 13 candidate repos deep-read (source inspected, not just READMEs); verdicts reflect what survived contact with the code.

This document does two jobs. First, it is a **sanity check** on the concept: does anything already do what fix-my-shot does — (a) grade shooting/exercise form or coach poses, (b) simulate a sports shot of any kind? Second, it is a **code-reuse shortlist** for (c): physics-engine, pose, and IK code we could actually build on for the MuJoCo-WASM pose editor and validity gate ([SPEC §5](../SPEC.md), [ADR-0003](../decisions/0003-engine-and-compute.md)). Verdicts are deliberately honest — where the landscape is thin, the thinness is itself the finding.

**Criteria**

- **(a)** similar functionality — form grading / pose coaching
- **(b)** sports shot simulation of any kind
- **(c)** physics-engine or pose/IK code we could actually reuse

## (a) Similar functionality — form grading / pose coaching

| Repo | License | Activity | What it is | What we take from it |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [yakupzengin/fitness-trainer-pose-estimation](https://github.com/yakupzengin/fitness-trainer-pose-estimation) | MIT | Active (pushed 2026-02) | Flask + MediaPipe fitness trainer: 18 exercises defined as **declarative YAML rules** — named joint-angle triples, FSM phases, severity-tiered feedback, 0–100 form score | The closest deployed instance of our principle-ranges pattern. Adopt the schema shape (named angles, `{condition, message, severity}` rules, deviation-from-ideal penalty aggregation) as JSON/TS; avoid its anti-patterns (pixel-space thresholds, `eval()` conditions) |
| [Areddy28/jumpshot-analyzer](https://github.com/Areddy28/jumpshot-analyzer) | **None** (all rights reserved) | Single-burst 2026-06, abandoned | Fully client-side jump-shot video analyzer (MediaPipe in-browser): phase detection + six rule-based feedback items with explicit numeric thresholds | Same product goal, different paradigm (video observation, not simulation). Its thresholds (knee 100–140°, release elbow ≥160°, lean ≤0.08, torso-normalized offsets) are a concrete cross-check for our principle ranges; its pure, unit-tested analysis layer and self-hosted-WASM loading pattern are architecture precedents. **No license — reimplement, never copy** |
| [stevenzchen/pose-trainer](https://github.com/stevenzchen/pose-trainer) | GPL-3.0 | Dead (2021, explicitly unmaintained) | Academic exercise-form grader: OpenPose keypoints → torso-normalized geometric rules → per-violation corrective feedback strings | The published pattern for "measure per principle, compare to range, emit specific fix." Its torso-length normalization trick and (pass, [feedback]) rule shape map onto our scorer. **GPL — patterns only** |
| [chonyy/AI-basketball-analysis](https://github.com/chonyy/AI-basketball-analysis) | **OpenPose noncommercial** (README's MIT badge is false) | Dormant since ~2022 | Flask app: ball/hoop detection + OpenPose over shot videos; reports elbow/knee/release-angle/release-time — **measures but never grades** | Confirms the community-consensus metric vocabulary and operational definitions (set-point = min elbow/knee angle during ball-in-hand; standard joint triples). Its raw-numbers-without-ranges output is exactly the gap we fill. **License trap — nothing reusable** |
| [srz08/basketball-shot-analysis](https://github.com/srz08/basketball-shot-analysis) | **None** | Dead (course project, 2023) | YOLOv5 + MediaPipe Streamlit shot analyzer; five metrics per shot. README's broad form checklist (grip, follow-through, footwork) is **unimplemented** | Only a confirmation that elbow = shoulder-elbow-wrist, knee = hip-knee-ankle; its release-phase heuristic is a cross-check. Selection rationale did not survive source inspection — a caution about README-driven landscape scans |

**Finding.** Every existing form grader observes recorded video via 2D pose estimation. **Nobody grades an editable, simulated pose** — the simulate-and-edit paradigm at the core of fix-my-shot is unoccupied. Just as telling: every grader's thresholds are hand-picked rules of thumb with **zero cited research**, none ranks fixes (flat rule violations at best), and the two basketball-specific entries are unlicensed or license-trapped single-burst projects. There is demonstrated demand for basketball form feedback (1.2k stars on chonyy, forks asking for grading) and a demonstrated failure mode: the video-analysis approach stalls exactly where credible thresholds and prioritized coaching should begin.

## (b) Sports shot simulation

| Repo | License | Activity | What it is | What we take from it |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [tailuge/billiards](https://github.com/tailuge/billiards) | **GPL-3.0** | Very active (pushed today; developed since 2015) | Mature fully in-browser billiards simulator: custom research-paper-derived ball physics (Han 2005, Alciatore, Mathavan, Stronge), Three.js, jest physics-regression suite, runs on a Raspberry Pi 4 | The one real proof point that **physics-first sports simulation works in the browser** within a tight perf budget. Patterns (GPL — read-only): validate physics by recreating figures from source papers in-browser (`src/diagram/*` → template for validating our principle ranges against biomechanics literature); fixed-timestep loop decoupled from render; event-sourced replay with URL-shareable state; Web-Worker parameter-tuning harness |

Near-misses that did not qualify: [dm_control](https://github.com/google-deepmind/dm_control) has multi-agent soccer and `ball_in_cup` (proves body+ball MuJoCo scenes, not shot simulation); [kineticstoolkit](https://github.com/kineticstoolkit/kineticstoolkit) ships tennis-serve/basketball-sprint *measured* C3D data, no simulation; the criterion-(a) video analyzers fit a 2D parabola to detected pixels, which is observation, not simulation.

**Finding.** Exactly one mature open-source browser shot simulator exists, it simulates rigid balls with bespoke physics, and it is GPL. **Nobody simulates the shooter's body.** Combined with (a), the two halves of fix-my-shot — simulated body and graded form — each exist in isolation and have never been joined. That validates the delivery bet (billiards proves the browser budget) while confirming the concept has no incumbent.

### Ranked shortlist with next actions

1. **[google-deepmind/mujoco](https://github.com/google-deepmind/mujoco)** — the committed substrate; best possible fit for (c).
   *Next actions:* `npm install @mujoco/mujoco@3.10.0` (no source build). Port `wasm/demo_app/app.ts` (~450 lines) as the render loop: `MjModel.from_xml_string` → `mj_step`/`mj_forward` → `mjv_updateScene` → Three.js mesh sync with geometry cache. Start the scene from `model/humanoid/humanoid.xml`. Confirm `mjv_applyPerturbPose`/`MjvPerturb` (bound, per `wasm/codegen/generated/bindings.cc`) as the drag primitive. Wrap Embind `.delete()` lifecycle in a helper from day one — manual memory management is the sharpest edge. Use `wasm/tests/bindings_test.ts` as executable API docs. Plan for COOP/COEP headers only if the multithreaded build is ever needed.
2. **[zalo/mujoco_wasm](https://github.com/zalo/mujoco_wasm)** — closest existing scaffolding for a MuJoCo-WASM + Three.js drag editor; vendor and own (MIT).
   *Next actions:* lift `src/utils/DragStateManager.js` (raycast body picking, grab-plane tracking, OrbitControls handoff), `src/mujocoUtils.js` (`getPosition`/`getQuaternion`/`toMujocoPos` z-up↔y-up conversion), and the paused-drag branch in `src/main.js` (write `qpos`/`mocap_pos`, then `mj_forward`) — the exact skeleton of drag-to-pose with physics revalidation. Known limit: it only drags the **root** free joint; articulated per-joint dragging is ours to build (see mink). Note the ISC-vs-MIT `package.json` inconsistency in ATTRIBUTIONS.
3. **[kevinzakka/mink](https://github.com/kevinzakka/mink)** — the math of the validity gate; port the algorithms, not the package.
   *Next actions:* transliterate to TS: `solve_ik.py` (QP assembly: weighted stacked residuals, LM damping, limits as inequalities), `limits/collision_avoidance_limit.py` (stops limbs passing through floor/ball/self), `limits/configuration_limit.py` (joint ranges as inequalities, not post-hoc clamps), and the task trio `frame_task` (dragged joint) + `posture_task` (stay near reference pose) + `com_task` (balance over base — directly SPEC's quasi-static balance criterion). Port `lie/se3.py`/`so3.py` as the minimal SE3/SO3 layer. Use `examples/humanoid_g1.py` task weights as the starting point and `tests/test_solve_ik.py` + `docs/background/derivations.rst` as correctness oracles. Pick/write a small dense QP solver in TS (the one genuinely new piece).
4. **[google-deepmind/dm_control](https://github.com/google-deepmind/dm_control)** — engine-native humanoid assets that load directly into the WASM build.
   *Next actions:* copy `dm_control/suite/humanoid.xml` joint definitions (hinge axes, degree ranges, damping/stiffness/armature, contact solref/solimp, floor plane) as the shooter skeleton; strip the RL motor actuators. Take `suite/common/*.xml` for scene dressing and `suite/ball_in_cup.xml` as the ball-freejoint pattern. Port `locomotion/walkers/rescale.py` for user-height scaling. Keep `humanoid_CMU_V2020.xml` (56-DoF) in reserve if wrist/hand fidelity is needed for release grading. Vendor `mjcf/schema.xml` as the machine-readable MJCF schema.
5. **[three.js CCDIKSolver](https://github.com/mrdoob/three.js)** — zero-cost drag-preview IK inside the renderer we already use.
   *Next actions:* import from `three/addons/animation/CCDIKSolver.js`; one chain per draggable end-effector with `rotationMin`/`rotationMax` from our joint ranges; copy the interaction pattern from `examples/webgl_animation_skinning_ik.html`. Treat its output strictly as a **proposal to the MuJoCo gate**, never as authority. Pin three ≥ the 2026-04 limitation-sign fix (PR #33386).
6. **[MyoHub/myo_sim](https://github.com/MyoHub/myo_sim)** — anatomical honesty upgrade for the gate.
   *Next actions:* extract joint `range` attributes from `arm/assets/myoarm_body.xml` and `hand/assets/myohand_body.xml` (shoulder girdle, elbow, wrist) to replace the stylized humanoid's ad-hoc limits; evaluate `myoarm_simple.xml` / `myobody_simpleupper.xml` rigid chains if the stylized skeleton proves too coarse; `myohand.xml` (23-DoF) is the candidate for finger-level backspin-mechanics contact, **only after** benchmarking mesh/DoF cost in WASM. Mind the MuJoCo 3.2.4 pin vs our 3.10 runtime.
7. **[kineticstoolkit](https://github.com/kineticstoolkit/kineticstoolkit)** — conventions for the measurement layer between sim state and scorer.
   *Next actions:* port the semantics of `geometry.py` `get_local_coordinates()` (parent-relative segment transform = the joint-angle input) and `get_angles()` (Euler-sequence conventions, documented anatomical ranges, gimbal-lock handling) into a small TS module; adopt their convention discipline (fixed sequence strings + documented ranges) as the contract between MuJoCo state and the principle scorer.
8. **[ZhUyU1997/open-pose-editor](https://github.com/ZhUyU1997/open-pose-editor)** — UX reference for the editing layer.
   *Next actions:* study `src/editor.ts` for the joint-sphere picking / TransformControls / orbit-conflict architecture; borrow the `src/poses/data.json` serialization shape; verify FBX hand/foot asset provenance before ever shipping those meshes. Reference only — its kinematics-only joint model conflicts with MuJoCo-owned state.

## Gaps — what nobody has built

Nobody has built fix-my-shot, and the gap is specific. (1) **No one grades form on an editable, physically valid simulated pose** — every form-grading tool observes video, every pose editor is kinematics-only with no physics authority, and the two never meet. (2) **No one ships research-cited principle ranges** — every threshold in every grader is a hand-picked rule of thumb, which is precisely the credibility gap our evidence-backed baseline targets (and the "baseline authority" risk in [SPEC §10](../SPEC.md) cuts both ways: it is the moat *because* it is the hard part). (3) **No browser-grade IK-on-MuJoCo exists** — mink proves the math on the desktop in Python, but the validity gate must be built, matching the spec's own flag that pose realizability is the least-studied link. (4) **No one ranks fixes** — existing tools emit flat rule violations; the leverage hierarchy has no precedent to copy *or* to compete with. The pieces all exist separately — a browser-proven engine, field-tested humanoid models, the gate math, and clear demand for graded basketball form (stars and forks on abandoned analyzers) — and no project has joined them. A thin landscape at the intersection means no incumbent and no prior art to lean on: the concept is differentiated, and every integration risk (WASM perf budget, the QP gate in TS, defensible ranges) is ours alone to retire.
